🧬 Understanding Our Skin

Our Skin 🫧

Skin is the largest organ in the human body and acts as the first line of defense against the environment. It protects us from bacteria, ultraviolet (UV) radiation, and harmful substances.

Skin cells continuously grow, divide, and replace old cells to maintain healthy skin.

⚠️

When skin cells grow uncontrollably due to genetic mutations or environmental factors, abnormal cell growth can occur — which may lead to SKIN CANCER.

⚠️ The Problem

Skin Cancer is
Sneaky! 😰

Millions of people get skin cancer every year, but many don't know what to look for. And seeing a doctor isn't always easy or affordable...

🗺️ How It Works

Super Easy! Just 5 Steps 🙌

Even kids can use it — that's kind of the whole point! Here's what happens when you open SkinGuard:

🟢

Press START!

Tap the big green "START" button to wake up SkinGuard and tell Tobi you're ready to go!

📷

Camera Turns On

Your camera activates automatically. Point it at the skin area you want to check — hold steady!

🧠

AI Goes to Work!

PictoBlox's Machine Learning engine analyzes the image in real-time, crunching patterns invisibly fast.

🏷️

Get Your Result

SkinGuard classifies the image as Melanoma, Carcinoma, Actinic Keratosis, or Normal Mole — instantly!

🐻

Tobi Tells You What to Do

Our friendly bear guide explains the result in plain language and gives you some recommendations for what to do next.

🔍 Detection Types

What Can SkinGuard Find? 🧬

SkinGuard is focused on 4 priority categories of skin findings, with a short explanation for each result.

🚨

Melanoma

Suspicious for melanoma
High Priority

A malignant skin tumor characterized by aggressive growth. Lesions commonly exhibit asymmetry, irregular borders, color variation, increasing diameter, or progressive changes over time.

⚠️

Carcinoma

Suspicious for skin carcinoma
Prompt Check Recommended

A malignant skin tumor, commonly basal cell carcinoma (BCC) or squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), associated with chronic UV exposure and presenting as persistent nodules, ulcerative lesions, or non-healing wounds.

☀️

Actinic Keratosis

Suspicious for sun-related skin lesion (precancerous)
Follow Up Recommended

A precancerous keratinocytic lesion caused by ultraviolet (UV) radiation exposure, appears as rough, scaly, or hyperkeratotic patches on sun-exposed areas of the skin.

✅

Normal Mole

No suspicious features detected (normal mole)
Low Concern

Normal moles are usually symmetrical, well-defined, uniformly pigmented, and stable in size and appearance over time.

We used PictoBlox — a super cool coding platform — to build SkinGuard from scratch using block-based coding and machine learning!

A kid-friendly coding platform with drag-and-drop blocks. Perfect for building AI apps without complex code!

Block Coding
